Dragons in the middle ages

Dragons are a common feature of the mythology of many nations of the world. In European folklore they are commonly depicted as amalgamations of dangerous beasts, typically reptilian with bat-like wings, sometimes with horns, crests, or the head of a large predatory animal such as a lion or wolf. They are dangerous, and breathe fire or poison out of their mouths.
