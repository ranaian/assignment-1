Overview

Orneta is a town in northern Poland, in the historical region of Prussia. It was founded in 1302 and features a fire-breathing Dragon on the coat of arms. According to the legend, the region was home to a Dragon that ate both livestock and civilians. It claimed the lives of many knights before being slain and buried in a cave, reportedly underneath the modern Town Hall.

Must-See Spots

  • St John the Baptist Church
  • Town Hall
  • A traditional restaurant
  • A public park
  • At this point I have to admit that Orneta is in the middle of nowhere and has little to do outside of the few historical buildings.

Visitor Tips

  • Orneta is a very small town, with a population of only 9000. Be polite and understanding.
  • They are unlikely to allow you to enter the cave underneath the town hall to look for Dragons.
  • St John's is an active parish, and visiting should be limited to non-service hours.
  • Orneta is an hour and a half drive from Gdansk, and anyone seriously planning to visit should probably think of Orneta as a day trip from there, and not a destination of its own.
Orneta is a small historical town of some small interest. Though probably not on the typical traveler's itenerary, it is primarily notable for its Dragon legend.
